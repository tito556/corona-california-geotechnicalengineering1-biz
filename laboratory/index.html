Corona’s geotechnical laboratory testing addresses the alluvial and weathered bedrock formations typical of western Riverside County. Compliance with ASTM International standards and Caltrans specifications governs every phase, from sample extrusion to final reporting. A thorough soil classification following USCS and AASHTO frameworks establishes the baseline for engineering design, while Atterberg limits define the plasticity range and shrink-swell potential of fine-grained soils common in the Santa Ana River corridor.

Residential subdivisions, commercial pads, and linear infrastructure projects rely on these analyses for foundation recommendations and earthwork control. The Proctor test determines maximum dry density and optimum moisture content for compacted fill, whereas the oedometer consolidation test quantifies settlement rates under structural loads. Together these procedures convert local soil variability into reliable design parameters.
